Literature Survey

Figure 1. Learning Management System Market Trends [1]




Figure 2. Smart Education and Learning Market Overview [2]

Early childhood is a critical period for developing communication, cognitive, and motor skills. Traditional Montessori methods, although effective in nurturing creativity and independence, often lack the personalized feedback necessary to address individual learning gaps in nursery school children [1]. Recent advances in machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) have opened new avenues to address these limitations. For instance, Gerosa et al. identified challenges in speech recognition for children due to variability in pitch and fluency, proposing child-specific ASR models to improve recognition rates [2]. Building upon this, Xu et al. introduced a DNN-based voice assistant that provides real-time feedback to enhance preschoolers' language skills in noisy environments [3].

Alharbi et al. highlighted the significance of adaptive learning systems powered by AI, which can personalize the learning journey, thereby improving engagement and language outcomes [4]. To support this, Sharma et al. proposed a computer vision system to monitor attention in online learning, which is highly relevant for tracking engagement in nursery-level educational platforms [5].

Finally, integrating risk assessment and personalized learning through facial expression analysis and attention tracking has shown potential in identifying early signs of disengagement or difficulty. Tools like EfficientNet and GazeML have been used to track behavioral patterns in real time, helping tailor learning pathways and enhance classroom safety [6].

These studies collectively underscore the promise of machine learning in revolutionizing early education, offering objective, scalable, and adaptive solutions that support the holistic development of nursery school children.

Research Gap

Following areas are the key research gaps identified in recent studies on early childhood education and technology integration:

Limited Speech Recognition Adapted for Young Children

Current speech recognition systems are primarily developed for adult users. These systems struggle with the high variability in pitch, pronunciation, and fluency of nursery-aged children, leading to inaccurate feedback and limited learning support.

Lack of Objective Drawing and Coloring Skill Evaluation

Traditional assessment of children's artistic abilities relies on subjective human judgment. There is a lack of AI-powered tools that provide real-time, objective, and personalized feedback on children's drawing and coloring efforts, limiting creativity-focused skill development.

Minimal Use of Risk and Attention Monitoring in Classrooms

Real-time tracking of student engagement and early identification of learning difficulties is largely unexplored in nursery school settings. Integrating image processing and behavior analytics for risk assessment remains an underdeveloped area.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How can machine learning be used to personalize learning and improve speaking, drawing, and cognitive skills in nursery school children?

Early childhood education in Sri Lanka often relies on traditional, one-size-fits-all teaching methods. These approaches do not effectively support the unique learning needs and developmental stages of each child. As a result, children may struggle with essential skills like speaking, drawing, and color recognition. Furthermore, there is limited use of real-time feedback or objective assessment to guide improvements. This gap highlights the need for a smart learning platform that integrates machine learning, voice recognition, and image processing. Such a system can offer personalized feedback, monitor student engagement, and assess skills like drawing and coloring with high accuracy. This research aims to develop an AI-driven solution that supports individual learning journeys, fosters creativity, and equips educators and parents with meaningful insights.

Proposed Solution

Our research introduces a Machine Learning-based Smart Learning Platform designed to enhance early childhood education through four key modules: Speaking Skills Development, Drawing Skills Enhancement, Personalized Learning and Risk Assessment, and AI-Driven Coloring Skill Development. The platform leverages advanced technologies such as speech recognition, image processing, and adaptive learning algorithms to provide personalized, real-time feedback tailored to each child's developmental needs. For speaking skills, the system analyzes pitch, loudness, and speech rate to offer corrective guidance, while the drawing module objectively evaluates artwork against predefined benchmarks to improve accuracy and creativity. The coloring module uses AI to assess color recognition and blending skills, delivering customized exercises to refine fine motor skills. Additionally, the platform incorporates facial analysis and engagement tracking to monitor learning progress and identify potential risks, ensuring a safe and adaptive learning environment. By integrating these technologies into Montessori education, the platform aims to create an interactive, data-driven system that fosters independence, creativity, and confidence in nursery school children, while providing educators and parents with actionable insights into each child's development.

Research Objectives

Enhancing Speaking Skills through Voice Recognition

The first objective is to improve the speaking abilities of nursery school children by implementing a voice recognition system. Using machine learning, the system analyzes speech features such as pitch, pronunciation, and speed to provide real-time, personalized feedback. This helps children practice language more effectively while enabling teachers to track progress and intervene when needed.

Evaluating and Improving Drawing Skills Using Image Processing

The second objective is to create a solution that evaluates children‚Äôs drawing skills by comparing their artwork with reference shapes using image processing. Techniques such as edge detection and shape matching are used to assess accuracy, proportion, and creativity. Feedback will be automatically generated to guide the child in skill improvement, fostering cognitive and fine motor development.

Developing AI-Powered Coloring Skill Assessment

The third objective is to assess coloring tasks performed by children using deep learning models like CNNs. The system identifies parameters such as color choice, stroke consistency, and area coverage. These metrics help determine the child‚Äôs understanding of color theory and motor control. Adaptive exercises will be recommended based on the assessment outcomes to enhance learning.

Personalized Learning and Risk Assessment through Engagement Monitoring

The fourth objective is to implement a personalized learning path and real-time risk assessment by tracking children's engagement levels through facial expressions and behavior analysis. Using computer vision and gaze-tracking algorithms, the system monitors attention span and emotional cues, ensuring each child receives tailored educational support and timely interventions when disengagement is detected.

Methodology

Figure 3. overall System diagram.

The proposed system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Improve Nursery school children's speaking skills using voice recognition machine learning techniques.
  2. Drawing skills improvement with simple drawing quizzes, using image processing technology
  3. AI-Powered Coloring Skill Development for Nursery school children.
  4. Personalized Learning and Risk Assessment System with image processing.

The Overall System Diagram illustrates a Smart Learning Platform that integrates Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) to enhance early childhood education. This system is designed to support nursery school children in four key areas: improved speaking skills, learning and risk assessment, drawing skills improvement, and coloring skill development. By leveraging AI-driven data collection, processing, and analysis, the platform provides personalized learning experiences that adapt to each child's abilities and progress

The system begins by gathering input from the four learning components. Each component corresponds to a specific skill set, such as speech improvement through voice interactions, behavioral assessments to monitor learning risks, and fine motor skill evaluation through drawing and coloring tasks. These interactions are recorded and processed by the Smart Learning Platform, ensuring that relevant data is collected for further analysis.
